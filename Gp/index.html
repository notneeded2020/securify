Dealing with Cyberbullying

A 2018 survey of children’s online behavior found that approximately 60% of children who use social media have witnessed some form of bullying, and that, for various reasons, most children ignored the behavior. If you are cyberbullied, here are some tips on how to deal with them.

Don’t retaliate and write a nasty post in response.

It will only make the situation worse.

Do not reread the offending comments.

This will only lead to obsession and further anger. Report the offending comments to a group moderator if possible.

Make a 30 second rule.

After you’ve written something , step away from the computer or phone for 30 seconds. When you come back, look at the message you wrote and ask yourself how you’d feel if someone wrote this to you.

Take a technology break.

Turn off your phone and computer for a night and enjoy doing something free from technology such as enjoying a nice meal, meditating, or taking a warm bath.

Dealing with Phishing attacks

Phishing scams are designed to trick people in handing over usernames and passwords, which can be used to access protected data, networks and systems. They are usually sent through emails. If you think you have been phished, then follow these steps.

Disconnect your device from the internet and any network it is linked to.

This’ll reduce the risk of the malware spreading through your system.

Perform a complete scan of your system using your anti-virus software.

This can be done offline, so ignore any pop-ups telling you to connect to the internet. If you find any malware, follow the software’s instructions on how to quarantine or remove the malicious files.

Change your details.

Often, phishing emails are used to steal personal information like passwords and bank details. If you think you’re at risk, you are best.

If you think financial information is at risk, alert your bank.

If you think financial information is at risk, alert your bank that your details may have been stolen so that they can monitor any suspicious activity.

Dealing with a D-Dos attack

A distributed denial-of-service (DDoS) attack is a malicious attempt to disrupt the normal traffic of a targeted server, service or network by overwhelming the target or its surrounding infrastructure with a flood of Internet traffic. Preventing a DDoS attack when malicious actors can launch over 1 Tbps at your servers is almost impossible, and that means that it is more than important than ever to understand how to stop a DDoS attack after it has started to affect your operations. Here are some tips to stop them.

Identify The D-DoS Attack Early

The sooner you can establish that problems with your website are due to a DDoS attack, the sooner you can stop the DDoS attack.

Overprovision Bandwidth

This may give you a few extra minutes to act before your resources are overwhelmed completely.

Call A D-DoS Mitigation Specialist

These organizations have large-scale infrastructure and use a variety of technologies, including data scrubbing, to help keep your website online.

Create A D-DoS Playbook

The best way to ensure that your organization reacts as quickly and effectively as possible to stop a DDoS attack is to create a playbook that documents in detail every step of a pre-planned response when an attack is detected.

Preventing a MITM Attack

Man-in-the-middle attacks (MITM) are a common type of cybersecurity attack that allows attackers to eavesdrop on the communication between two targets. The attack takes place in between two legitimately communicating hosts, allowing the attacker to “listen” to a conversation they should normally not be able to listen to, hence the name “man-in-the-middle.” Here are some ways how you can prevent it fom happening.

Strong WEP/WPA Encryption on Access Points

Having a strong encryption mechanism on wireless access points prevents unwanted users from joining your network just by being nearby.

Virtual Private Network

VPNs can be used to create a secure environment for sensitive information within a local area network. They use key-based encryption to create a subnet for secure communication.

Public Key Pair Based Authentication

Public key pair based authentication like RSA can be used in various layers of the stack to help ensure whether the things you are communicating with are actually the things you want to be communicating with.

Force HTTPS

HTTPS can be used to securely communicate over HTTP using public-private key exchange. This prevents an attacker from having any use of the data he may be sniffing.

Detecting a DNS Tunneling Attack

DNS Tunneling is a method of cyber attack that encodes the data of other programs or protocols in DNS queries and responses. DNS tunneling enables these cybercriminals to insert malware or pass stolen information into DNS queries, creating a covert communication channel that bypasses most firewalls. Here is how you can detect a DNS Tunneling Attack.

Payload Analysis method

With this method the defender looks for unusual data being sent back and forth using statistical techniques

Traffic Analysis method

With this method the defender looks at the number of requests to a DNS domain and compares it against average usage.

Using Monitoring Utilities

dnsHunter and reassemble_dns are some utilities that are useful for detecting tunneling attacks

DNS Tunneling utilities for pen testing

If you want to do your own pen testing to see how well your company can detect and respond, there are a few utilities available like, Iodine, OzymanDNS, DNSCat2.

Dealing with a SQL Attack

The attack takes place in between two legitimately communicating hosts, allowing the attacker to “listen” to a conversation they should normally not be able to listen to, hence the name “man-in-the-middle.”

Validate User Inputs

First, identify the essential SQL statements and establish a whitelist for all valid SQL statements, leaving unvalidated statements out of the query.

Sanitize Data By Limiting Special Characters

SQLi attackers can use unique character sequences to take advantage of a database, sanitizing data not to allow string concatenation is critical.

Enforce Prepared Statements And Parameterization

By defining all SQL code involved with queries, or parameterization, you can distinguish between user input and code.

Raise Virtual Or Physical Firewalls

Firewalls today, including NGFW and FWaaS offerings, have both a comprehensive set of default rules and the ease to change configurations as needed. If a patch or update has yet to be released, WAFs can be handy.

How to make a safe, secure and strong passwords

We need passwords for credit card accounts, social media accounts, work, and any number of other things. Despite how prevalent they are, and despite how important they are, a lot of us still have trouble creating good passwords. If you follow the tips listed here, you'll be able to create strong passwords that will help keep you safe.

Don’ts:-

  • Don't make short passwords. To be safe, make your password atleast 14 characters long.
  • Don't keep a password for too long. Change them often.
  • Don't make a password that's easy to guess. Some passwords are super easy to guess because they get used all the time. like, 12345.
  • Don't store your password where it can be easily found.If you've written your passwords down and left them where you can easily get to them, chances are good someone else can easily get to them, too.

Do's:-

  • Do make passwords easy to remember. Make them long, but easy to remember.
  • Do use a password manager. Password managers save the passwords for you, so you don't have to remember them. We do that here at Securify, all you have to do is sign up.
  • Do use a different password for every site. This way, if a hacker does get one of your passwords, he won't be able to crack the others.
  • Do play with your security question answers. Sometimes, some sites may ask you personal questions without you realizing it. So it's better to make up an answer to the questions asked.
